Reliability and how we achieve

  • The probability of a system or component performing its required functions under stated conditions for a specified period of time.
  • Three key words of reliability: failure, time and operation condition.
  • Reliability engineering attempt to study, characterize, measure, and analyze the failure and repair of systems in order to improve their operational use by Increasing their design life, eliminating or reducing the likelihood of failures (safe/unsafe failure), and reducing downtime (or increasing availability).

Product life cycle costing

  • Life Cycle Costs (LCC) are the total costs for the acquisition, operation and maintenance during the life cycle of a system.
  • The procedure for determining the LCC is carried out in such a way that a system and / or a project is split into its elementary components to which costs can be allocated (personnel, material, operation, etc.).
  • For the life cycle of the overall system, the focus of this LCC calculation is on the maintenance costs. Maintenance is understood to mean preventive and corrective maintenance measures.

SWOT analysis

  • A SWOT analysis helps find the best match between environmental trends (opportunities and threats) and internal capabilities.
  • A Strength is a resource or capacity the organization can use effectively to achieve its objectives.
  • A Weakness is a limitation, fault, or defect in the organization that will keep it from achieving its objectives.
  • An Opportunity is any favorable situation in the organization's environment. It is usually a trend or change of some kind or an overlooked need that increases demand for a product or service and permits the firm to enhance its position by supplying it.
  • A Threat is any unfavorable situation in the organization's environment that is potentially damaging to its strategy. The threat may be a barrier, a constraint, or anything external that might cause problems, damage or injury.

FEMA

  • A FMEA is a design tool that helps to foresee all the possible the failure modes and makes the product robust.
  • A Design FMEA concerns all the failures that are anticipated due to the design failures and sets suitable mitigation plan according to the cost-benefit analysis.
  • Process FMEA concerns all the failures that are anticipated due manufacturing processes, this process can invoke requirement of suitable jigs and fixtures in order to eliminate defects whilst achieving optimum quality.
